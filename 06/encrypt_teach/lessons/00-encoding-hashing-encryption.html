Encoding ≠ Hashing ≠ Encryption

Before any cipher, one confusion has to go. People say a string is “encrypted” when it is merely encoded. The difference is not cosmetic — it is the whole question of who can undo it. That question introduces the single word the rest of this site turns on: the key.

How it works

Three things happen to your text — only one keeps a secret

Type a message. Watch each transform. Notice which ones hand the original straight back with no key at all.

ROT13 · encoding in disguisereversible by anyone

It looks scrambled. It is a Caesar shift of 13, its own inverse, and it has no key. This is the trap.

SHA-256 · hashingnot reversible at all

A fingerprint. There is no “decode” button, for anyone, ever — even the person who made it.

AES-GCM · encryptionreversible only with the key
