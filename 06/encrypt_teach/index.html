Every cipher is a tombstone for the one before it.

This is not a museum of ciphers in glass cases. It is one long argument. Each method on this site exists because you are about to break the previous one — yourself, in the browser.

The chain of ciphers and their breaks

  1. STOP 0 · GROUND-CLEARING
    Encoding ≠ Encryption
    Break · Base64 reverses with no key, in front of you.

    Forces the word that runs the rest of the site: key — and the question of who holds it.

    Caesar
    Break · brute-force all 25 shifts at once.

  5. STOP 2 · THE BIG IDEA
    Keyed substitution
    Break · frequency analysis. 2⁸⁸ keys and it still falls — you crack it by hand.

    Forced flattening the letter statistics: many alphabets, not one.

  6. │
    ▼ the key repeats
  7. STOP 3
    Vigenère
    Break · Kasiski & Friedman find the period; it collapses into Caesars.

    Forced a key with no period — as long as the message.

  8. │
    ▼ reuse the key once
  9. STOP 4
    One-time pad
    Break · two-time pad — reuse the key and it cancels; crib-drag both messages out.

    Perfect but unusable. Forced short keys stretched by a pseudo-random permutation.

  10. │
    ▼ identical blocks show
  11. STOP 5
    Block ciphers & ECB
    Break · the ECB penguin — correct AES, correct key, the picture survives.

    Forced modes that hide position.

  12. │
    ▼ ciphertext is malleable
  13. STOP 6
    Modes & malleability
    Break · flip one ciphertext bit, flip one plaintext bit — without the key.

    Encryption is not authentication. Forced AEAD.

  14. │
    ▼ but how do we share a key?
  15. STOP 7
    Diffie–Hellman
    Break · a passive eavesdropper fails — but a man in the middle walks straight in.

    Forced a way to bind a key to an identity.

  16. │
    ▼ who are you talking to?
  17. STOP 8
    RSA
    Break · textbook RSA is deterministic — encrypt twice, get the same thing, and lose IND-CPA.

    Forced a formal adversary model, and padding (OAEP).

  18. │
    ▼ theory meets the machine
  19. STOP 9 · THE REAL WORLD
    Don't roll your own — the mechanism
    Break · measure a timing side channel live; watch a biased RNG make correlated keys.

    Not a commandment. A demonstrated reason.

How to read this

Take the stops in order — each one only makes sense as an answer to the last one's failure. Every lesson has the same three beats:

How it works Encrypt and decrypt it yourself.
How it breaks Run the attack with your own hands.
What it forced The design the break demanded.

Toy and classical ciphers are implemented in readable JavaScript you can open in src/. Anything modern — AES, SHA-256, HMAC, ECDH — uses the browser's real Web Crypto; it is never hand-rolled. Teaching implementations carry a standing warning that they are not for production.

Everything runs locally in your browser. No text you type here leaves the page.
